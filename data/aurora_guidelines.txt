Aurora Activewear – Brand Guidelines (v1.0)

1. Brand Essence
- Aurora Activewear exists to help ambitious professionals feel confident, energized, and ready to move.
- Our positioning: premium, performance-focused athleisure with an approachable price point.
- Core values: performance, confidence, inclusivity, and sustainability.

2. Brand Voice and Tone
- Our brand voice is bold, energetic, and professional.
- We speak with confidence backed by data, never with hype or exaggeration.
- We avoid slang that will age quickly; we prefer clear, modern language.
- Tone guidelines:
  - Launch and promotional campaigns: upbeat, aspirational, slightly playful.
  - Performance or quarterly reports: direct, analytical, and recommendation-driven.
  - Customer-facing summaries: encouraging, solutions-focused, never blaming.

3. Target Audience
- Primary audience: urban professionals aged 25–40 who work in hybrid or office roles.
- Audience attributes:
  - Time-poor but health-conscious.
  - Comfortable buying premium products online.
  - Heavy users of Instagram and YouTube; moderate users of Facebook; low engagement on LinkedIn.
- We prioritize creatives and placements that feature:
  - Realistic body types.
  - Active lifestyle scenes (commuting, post-work gym, weekend activities).
  - Clear product benefits (comfort, fit, performance fabric).

4. Performance and Media Goals
- Our target overall ROAS is 4.0 across paid media.
- Minimum acceptable ROAS is 3.2 for experimental campaigns and 3.5 for always-on campaigns.
- Our target blended CTR is 2.0% or higher for prospecting campaigns and 2.5% or higher for retargeting.
- We prioritize quality of traffic over raw volume; it is acceptable to reduce impressions if ROAS and CTR improve.
- We are comfortable increasing spend by 20–30% week-over-week when:
  - ROAS is 4.0 or higher AND
  - CTR is 2.0% or higher AND
  - Conversion volume is stable or increasing.

5. Channel Priorities
- We prioritize Instagram over LinkedIn for both paid and organic activity.
- Channel hierarchy for paid campaigns:
  1) Instagram (Feed, Reels, Stories)
  2) Facebook (Feed, Stories)
  3) Google Search (brand + high-intent non-brand)
  4) TikTok (test budgets only)
  5) LinkedIn (not a focus unless explicitly requested by B2B partners)
- When performance is strong on Instagram and Facebook but weak on LinkedIn:
  - Reallocate budget away from LinkedIn.
  - Do not force LinkedIn spend just for coverage.

6. Creative and Messaging Guidelines
- Mandatory elements in performance ads:
  - Clear product hero image or video in the first 2 seconds.
  - Simple benefit-led headline (e.g., “All-day comfort that moves with you”).
  - “Shop now” or equivalent call-to-action.
- Avoid:
  - Overly crowded layouts.
  - Long paragraphs of copy in images.
  - Stock photography that does not reflect our real customer base.
- Copy tone:
  - Bold but honest claims (e.g., “Thousands of reviewers rate Aurora 4.8/5” only if true).
  - Focus on how the product fits into the customer’s day rather than abstract lifestyle slogans.

7. Measurement and Reporting Standards
- Standard KPIs for campaign reports:
  - Spend, Impressions, Clicks, CTR, Conversions, Revenue, ROAS, CPC, CPM.
- We expect all quarterly reports to clearly highlight:
  - Top 3 performing channels by ROAS.
  - Underperforming channels relative to our target ROAS of 4.0.
  - At least one clear red flag and one clear opportunity.
- We prefer recommendations that:
  - Are specific (“Shift 15% of budget from LinkedIn to Instagram Reels”) rather than generic.
  - Are grounded in both numbers and brand context (voice, audience, and channel priorities).

8. Risk Tolerance and Guardrails
- We tolerate short-term ROAS dips during controlled tests on new channels or creatives, as long as:
  - Test budgets are capped at 20% of total spend.
  - There is a clear hypothesis and success metric.
- We do NOT accept sustained ROAS below 3.0 on always-on campaigns without a remediation plan.
- We will pause any audience or channel that consistently underperforms for 4 consecutive weeks.

End of Aurora Activewear Brand Guidelines.
